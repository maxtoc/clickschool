Bill Mollison - Global Gardener

Bill Mollison was an Australian researcher, author, scientist, teacher and naturalist who is best known for co-founding the permaculture movement. He was born on August 4, 1928, in Stanley, Tasmania, Australia, and passed away on September 24, 2016.

Mollison was a pioneer in the field of permaculture, which is a design philosophy for creating sustainable human habitats. He developed this concept in the 1970s and wrote several books on the subject, including "Permaculture: A Designer's Manual."

Mollison traveled extensively, teaching and promoting permaculture throughout the world. He received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to the environmental movement, including the Right Livelihood Award, also known as the "Alternative Nobel Prize."

Mollison's legacy continues to influence the field of sustainable design and his ideas have been adopted by people around the world who are working to create a more sustainable future.


Years of Bill Mollison's active years:

  • 1970s - Mollison co-founded the permaculture movement and wrote his first book on the subject.
  • 1980s - Mollison continued to travel and teach permaculture, establishing centers and training programs throughout the world.
  • 1990s - Mollison wrote several more books on permaculture and received numerous awards for his work in sustainable design.
  • 2000s - Mollison remained active in the permaculture movement, continuing to teach and inspire others through his lectures and books.
  • 2010s - Mollison continued to be an influential figure in the permaculture community, with his ideas and teachings having a lasting impact on the field.
